# Humanize Floor Plan (Humanização de Planta Baixa)

Add realistic human figures, scale references, and contextual elements to architectural floor plans for professional visualization and presentation.

## When to Use This Skill

- Preparing floor plan renderings for client presentations
- Adding scale context to architectural visualizations
- Generating realistic floor plan images with occupancy context
- Creating variations of humanized floor plans
- Validating spatial proportions and human scale
- Preparing designs for 3D rendering or AI enhancement

## Key Workflow

### Step 1: Analyze Floor Plan Requirements

**Input Assessment:**
- Examine the floor plan's dimensions, scale, and layout
- Identify key spaces (living areas, corridors, entrances)
- Determine occupancy patterns and human placements
- Calculate human scale proportions (avg. height: 5.5-6 ft / 1.7-1.8 m)
- Note furniture and spatial context

**Questions to Ask:**
- What's the scale of the floor plan? (1:100, 1:50, etc.)
- How many occupants should be shown?
- What are they doing? (sitting, walking, working, etc.)
- What's the intended use? (residential, commercial, hospitality, etc.)
- What viewing angle? (top-down, perspective, mixed)

### Step 2: Define Human Placement Strategy

**Placement Considerations:**
- Walking paths and circulation zones
- Furniture arrangement and seating areas
- Standing room vs. seated areas
- Activity zones (kitchen, bedroom, workspace)
- Natural entry and exit points
- Social grouping and conversation distances

**Diversity & Realism:**
- Vary figure poses (standing, sitting, bending, walking)
- Use different clothing and appearance styles
- Consider age/gender diversity
- Add contextual activities (reading, cooking, working, etc.)
- Include shadows and movement suggest at typical times

### Step 3: Choose Implementation Path

**Option A: AI Image Generation**
- Use DALL-E, Midjourney, or Stable Diffusion
- Prompt with floor plan dimensions, space descriptions, and human placement details
- Specify style (photorealistic, architectural rendering, etc.)
- Generate variations with different occupancy scenarios

**Option B: 3D Rendering (Blender/SketchUp)**
- Import or recreate floor plan geometry
- Add human figure models/rigs
- Set up lighting and camera angles
- Render with realistic textures and shadows
- Export high-quality images

**Option C: Design Tools (Figma, Illustrator)**
- Use vector/raster figures from libraries
- Scale accurately to floor plan proportions
- Layer human figures over floor plan
- Add shadows, lighting effects
- Create multiple variations

**Option D: Architectural Tools (Revit, ArchiCAD)**
- Use built-in figure symbols and components
- Configure occupancy levels and placements
- Generate 2D floor plan views with figures
- Export renderings

### Step 4: Generate or Place Figures

**AI Generation Workflow:**
```
Detailed prompt template:
"[Scale] floor plan of [space type] [dimensions]. Show [number] people [activity].
Realistic architectural rendering, human scale, professional visualization.
Top-down view with soft shadows. [Specific placements]. Professional quality."
```

**Manual Placement Workflow:**
- Use figure templates scaled to floor plan ratio
- Position in logical activity zones
- Adjust overlaps and layering
- Add depth with shadows
- Apply lighting and effects

### Step 5: Validate & Refine

**Scale Validation:**
- Verify human figures match floor plan scale
- Check door and furniture proportions against humans
- Ensure walkways accommodate figure widths
- Validate spatial distances

**Realism Check:**
- Confirm natural poses and positioning
- Check lighting consistency
- Verify shadow directions
- Assess overall composition and balance
- Review for architectural accuracy

**Refinement Options:**
- Regenerate with adjusted prompts (AI)
- Reposition or replace figures (manual)
- Adjust lighting and effects
- Generate variations for comparison
- Export in required format

### Step 6: Export & Present

**Output Considerations:**
- Resolution and DPI for intended use
- File format (PNG, JPG, PDF, SVG)
- Watermark or branding if needed
- Multiple variations or scenarios
- Annotations or dimension callouts
- Comparison images

## Reference: Standard Human Dimensions

| Measurement | Feet | Meters | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standing Height** | 5.5-6 | 1.7-1.8 | Average adult |
| **Seated Height** | 2.5-3 | 0.75-0.9 | Torso only |
| **Shoulder Width** | 1.5-1.75 | 0.45-0.53 | Personal space |
| **Personal Space** | 1.5-4 | 0.45-1.2 | Depends on context |
| **Walkway Min** | 3 | 0.9 | One person |
| **Walkway Comfortable** | 4-5 | 1.2-1.5 | Two people |

## Tips for Realistic Humanization

✓ **Variety**: Mix standing, sitting, walking poses  
✓ **Context**: Add activities that explain presence (cooking, reading, working)  
✓ **Shadows**: Include realistic shadows for depth  
✓ **Scale**: Double-check all dimensions against floor plan scale  
✓ **Proportions**: Verify human figures don't obstruct key features unnecessarily  
✓ **Lighting**: Match lighting direction with floor plan perspective  
✓ **Multiple Options**: Generate/create 2-3 variations before finalizing  

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Figures too large/small | Recalculate scale ratio; verify floor plan dimensions |
| Unnatural poses | Regenerate with specific pose requests; use pose references |
| Poor lighting | Adjust light direction; add/remove shadows; check perspective |
| Scale inconsistencies | Use architectural figure libraries; double-check ratios |
| AI output too surreal | Refine prompt with "photorealistic," "architectural," "professional" |
| Not enough detail | Increase resolution; use 3D rendering instead of AI |
